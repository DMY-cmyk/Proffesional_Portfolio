What a reviewer sees

A creative-portfolio aesthetic (dark, animated aurora, gold shimmer on name, custom cursor) wrapped around a finance claim. The two signals contradict each other within 3 seconds.

No date of graduation, no current institution, no current role visible above the fold. The reviewer can't place you.

CTA "See my work" — but there's no portfolio of deliverables. It links to a near-empty Certifications section.

What a reviewer needs

A clear status ribbon: Bachelor of Accounting @ STIE YKPN · Operations Officer @ Kolosal AI · Jakarta.

A CTA that leads to substance: "Read my research" or "Download CV".

LinkedIn and email accessible immediately — not at the bottom of a long scroll.

Snapshot: current skills section

The single most universally disliked pattern in HR-reviewed portfolios. Self-rated percentages on technical skills.

Technical Skills

Financial Reporting90%
Tax Compliance85%
Auditing80%
Budgeting & Forecasting75%
Financial Analysis85%

Why this fails

The percentages are unverifiable. An 80% in auditing means nothing to a Big 4 recruiter. Self-rating is read as either naive or self-aggrandizing.

Every candidate looks the same. "Auditing 80%" on every early-career portfolio is noise, not signal.

No proof is attached. A reviewer has no way to validate the claim.

Fix: replace bars with tag clouds grouped by category, and attach proof (years used, at what firm/course, and for what outcome). See the mockup in any Direction file.

The critical issues

● Criticalsrc/content/research/index.json

Placeholder research entry on live public site

Literal content: "Placeholder Research Paper Title" with abstract "This is a placeholder abstract for a research paper exploring financial topics."

Impact: Immediate disqualification for an academic reviewer (audience D). Signals the site is abandoned or unmaintained.

Fix: Replace with real research content before the redesign ships, OR remove the entry and hide the research section until real content exists.

● Criticalsrc/content/skills.json · src/components/sections/skills-section.tsx

Self-rated skill percentages

All 15 skills across Technical / Tools / Soft categories have numeric percentages (65%–90%). This is an HR-reviewed anti-pattern — especially in finance where credentials are verifiable and numbers are earned.

Impact: Universal credibility damage across all reviewer types.

Fix: Replace with categorized tag clouds + proof (credential, years of use, where applied).

● Criticalsrc/app/page.tsx

Research buried in a sub-page, not on homepage

Homepage order: Hero → Profile → Timeline → Certifications → Skills → Contact. Research lives at /research, reached only by a nav-link click.

Impact: For audience D (grad school), research is the single highest-leverage content. Hiding it is self-sabotage.

Fix: Add a Research section between Hero and Profile (or between Profile and Timeline), with thesis as a featured card and other work below.

● Highsrc/app/layout.tsx · src/components/motion/*

Aesthetic mismatch: creative-portfolio visuals on a finance site

Aurora canvas background + custom cursor + particle effects + gold shimmer gradient on name + avatar ambient glow pulse. All of these are motion-heavy and signal "creative / design / developer portfolio."

Impact: Finance/accounting HR and academic reviewers typically read this as doesn't understand industry norms.

Fix: Remove aurora, custom cursor, particles, shimmer, glow. Keep subtle scroll fade-in only. The restraint itself becomes a credibility signal.

● Highsrc/content/education.json

Thesis reduced to a single bullet

The thesis "The Effect of Sustainability Report Disclosure on Firm Value in the Manufacturing Sector" appears as one of three bullets in Education. It's the strongest academic artifact and gets no dedicated space.

Fix: Promote thesis to a dedicated featured card in the Research section — with abstract, methodology, tags, advisor (if shareable), and optional PDF.

● Highsrc/components/sections/hero-section.tsx

No immediate credibility proof in hero

Hero shows name + title ("Accounting & Finance Professional") + tagline + 2 CTAs + avatar. No date, institution, current role, location, or LinkedIn link above the fold.

Fix: Add a compact status line: Bachelor of Accounting @ STIE YKPN · Operations Officer @ Kolosal AI · Jakarta. Add LinkedIn + email icons in hero.

● Mediumsrc/components/sections/hero-section.tsx

"See my work" points to a sparse section

Primary hero CTA reads "See my work" and scrolls to the Certifications section, which contains one entry.

Fix: Change to "Read my research" scrolling to the new Research section, OR "Download CV" as a file download.

● Mediumsrc/content/certifications.json · awards.json · courses.json

Thin sections look thinner in a grid

Certifications: 1 item. Awards: 0. Courses: 2. A grid layout emphasizes the emptiness.

Fix: Consolidate into a single "Credentials" section. Use a compact vertical list. Add more real entries.

● Mediumsrc/content/contact.json

Personal socials mixed with professional

Contact includes LinkedIn, GitHub, Instagram, TikTok. For audience D+E, Instagram/TikTok dilute the professional signal.

Fix: Keep LinkedIn + email + optional GitHub. Remove Instagram/TikTok from the professional portfolio (can live on a separate "personal" page if desired).

● Lowsrc/styles/globals.css

Italic-only display type

Instrument Serif in italic, weight 400, is the sole display choice. Italic for all display is unusual and harder to scan at large sizes.

Fix: Use italic sparingly (for emphasis like thesis title), or swap to a non-italic scholarly serif.
