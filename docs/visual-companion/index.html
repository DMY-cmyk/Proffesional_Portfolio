Summary of audit findings

The current portfolio is engineered well, but its aesthetic and information hierarchy work against the owner's credibility for the intended audiences. Three issues will disqualify the site at first glance by a real reviewer:

Placeholder research entry on the live site. Instant disqualification for a grad-school reviewer.
Self-rated skill percentages (90%, 85%…). Widely distrusted; active credibility damage for finance/audit HR.
Research buried in a sub-page, not on the homepage. For audience D, this is the single highest-leverage content, hidden.
Aesthetic tone doesn't match the claimed role. Aurora, custom cursor, particles, shimmer — reads as creative portfolio, not finance.
Thesis buried as a single bullet. Should be the featured research card.
No immediate credibility proof in the hero. No dates, institution, or current role visible above the fold.
"See my work" CTA points to a near-empty Certifications section.
Sections look thin. Awards empty, 1 cert, 2 courses. Needs consolidation and more real entries.
