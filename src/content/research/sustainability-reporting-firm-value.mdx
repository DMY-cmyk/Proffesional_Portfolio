## Introduction

This research investigates whether sustainability report disclosure influences firm value among manufacturing companies listed on the Indonesia Stock Exchange (Bursa Efek Indonesia/BEI) during the 2021–2024 period. The study was motivated by inconsistent findings in prior research regarding the relationship between sustainability disclosure and firm value.

## Research Questions

1. Does the disclosure of economic aspects in sustainability reports affect firm value?
2. Does the disclosure of environmental aspects in sustainability reports affect firm value?
3. Does the disclosure of social aspects in sustainability reports affect firm value?

## Methodology

A quantitative approach was employed using secondary data from 37 manufacturing companies selected through purposive sampling. The data were analyzed using panel data regression with the assistance of EViews 12.

## Key Findings

The results show that sustainability report disclosures across economic, environmental, and social dimensions have a **negative but insignificant** effect on firm value. This indicates that sustainability disclosure has not succeeded in enhancing firm value and is still perceived as an additional cost burden by market participants.

## Recommendations

- Companies should improve the quality and relevance of data in their sustainability reports
- Regulators are encouraged to strengthen reporting standards to make them clearer and more useful for investors
- Future research should explore mediating variables and industry-specific effects
